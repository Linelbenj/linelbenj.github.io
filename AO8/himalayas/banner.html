Mt. Himalaya

The Himalayas are the highest mountain range on Earth, forming a dramatic arc across Asia that stretches over 2,400 km. They serve as a monumental border between the Indian subcontinent and the Tibetan Plateau.

Countries the Himalayas Span

  • Nepal
  • India
  • Bhutan
  • China (Tibet)
  • Pakistan

Highest Peaks

The Himalayas boast over 110 peaks taller than 7,000 meters. The crown jewel is:

“Mount Everest” — rising 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet), the highest point on Earth.

Spiritual Significance

Known as the "Abode of Snow" in Sanskrit, the Himalayas are sacred in Hinduism and Buddhism. Countless temples, monasteries, and meditation caves are hidden within these remote, serene peaks.

Adventure & Nature

The Himalayas attract travelers for their natural beauty and adventure opportunities. Activities include:

  • Trekking (e.g., Everest Base Camp, Annapurna Circuit)
  • High-altitude camping
  • Photography of glaciers, forests, and wildlife
